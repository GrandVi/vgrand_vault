According to the official SCOR 2030 manual, the rear suspension is designed for optimal performance with **28% to 30% sag**.
Because the SCOR 2030 frame can be set up with two different shock lengths depending on your rear wheel travel, the exact measurement in millimeters will depend on your shock stroke.
Here is the breakdown of the exact sag measurements for the rear shock:

| Setup | Shock Stroke | 28% Sag | 30% Sag |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stock (120mm travel)** | 47.5mm | 13.3mm | 14.25mm |
| **Modified (130mm travel)** | 52.5mm | 14.7mm | 15.75mm |
### Front Fork Setup
While SCOR specifically dictates the rear sag for their Lower-Link driven suspension, the 2030 comes equipped with a 140mm fork. For the front suspension:
 * **Recommended Sag:** Aim for **15% to 20%** sag depending on your riding style.
 * **Measurement:** On a 140mm fork, this translates to roughly **21mm to 28mm** of compression. 70 to 60 psi
If you prefer the 2030 to feel more like a snappy cross-country bike for pedaling efficiency, lean toward the 28% rear / 15% front end of the spectrum. If you want to tap into its "mini-enduro" capabilities on descents, aim closer to 30% in the rear and 20% up front.
